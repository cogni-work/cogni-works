**Trend Overview** — Engie's acquisition of UK Power Networks for £10.5 billion in 2026 signals an accelerating consolidation trend among European network operators [youarecurrent](https://youarecurrent.com/2026/03/03/aes-announced-acquisition-agreement/). **Implications** — Consolidation drives digital integration needs across merged utility portfolios, creating demand for unified data platforms and harmonized operational systems.

**Recommended Actions** — 1. Evaluate consolidation opportunities in European network markets. 2. Develop digital integration playbooks for post-merger harmonization. 3. Build modular platform architectures that scale across merged entities.

#### 3. Grid-Enhancing Technologies

**Trend Overview** — Grid-enhancing technologies such as dynamic line rating (DLR), advanced power flow control (APFC), and topology optimization can unlock significant additional capacity from existing infrastructure. GETs could free enough hosting capacity to connect 1,200–1,600 GW of projects stuck in queues worldwide [IEA](https://www.iea.org/reports/electricity-2026/grids). By mid-2025, at least 14 US states had enacted legislation requiring utilities to evaluate GETs [Bipartisan Policy Center](https://bipartisanpolicy.org/issue-brief/unlocking-the-potential-of-grid-enhancing-technologies-pathways-to-widespread-adoption/). The DOE has rolled out $1.9 billion in funding for grid upgrades [Renewable Energy World](https://www.renewableenergyworld.com/power-grid/grid-modernization/breaking-doe-rolls-out-1-9b-funding-opportunity-for-urgently-needed-grid-upgrades/).

**Implications** — GETs enable 10–15% capacity increases without physical network expansion, dramatically improving capital efficiency.

**Opportunities** — Utilities deploying GETs first gain competitive advantage in connecting new loads and reducing congestion costs.

**Recommended Actions** — 1. Evaluate DLR sensors and advanced power flow control systems. 2. Integrate GET capabilities into grid planning processes. 3. Develop business cases for GET deployment at congestion points.

#### 4. European €173B Investment Plan

**Trend Overview** — The top 40 European utilities will invest approximately €173 billion in 2026, a 6% increase over 2025 [ING Think](https://think.ing.com/articles/energy-european-utilities-2026-steady-currents/). Pure network utilities are increasing capex by 12%, bringing total investment for the top 20 operators to approximately €59 billion. Network utilities' capex represents 164% of their EBITDA on average in 2026.

**Implications** — Investment levels exceeding EBITDA indicate utilities are leveraging debt and regulated asset base growth to finance massive modernization programs.

**Opportunities** — Technology and service providers can capture significant share of €173 billion investment wave through digital solutions for grid modernization.

**Recommended Actions** — 1. Align technology roadmaps with European utility capex priorities. 2. Develop financing models for capex-intensive grid projects. 3. Build partnerships with network operators for co-investment.

#### 4. Performance-Based Regulation

**Trend Overview** — Performance-based regulation (PBR) is expanding from the US to Europe, rewarding efficiency outcomes rather than cost-plus tariffs. 28+ US states have adopted PBR frameworks [Strategy&](https://www.strategyand.pwc.com/gx/en/industries/power-utilities/utilities-innovation.html). EU regulators are studying adoption, and Germany is exploring a pilot framework.

**Implications** — PBR shifts utility incentives from capital spending to operational outcomes, requiring sophisticated performance measurement and reporting capabilities.

**Opportunities** — Utilities with strong digital operations and data capabilities are better positioned to benefit from performance-based incentives.

**Recommended Actions** — 1. Develop PBR-ready performance metrics and reporting. 2. Build efficiency measurement capabilities. 3. Engage regulators on pilot framework design.
