# B2B Open-Source Portfolio Taxonomy

Single source of truth for the B2B Open-Source (COSS) Portfolio framework — 8 dimensions (0-7), 50 categories.

## Purpose

The framework provides a MECE structure for analyzing commercial open-source software companies. Dimension 0 covers provider business profile metrics; Dimensions 1-7 cover the unique portfolio characteristics of open-source business models: the OSS projects themselves, enterprise platform wrappers, cloud/managed services, developer ecosystem, support subscriptions, professional services, and licensing strategy.

**Why a dedicated taxonomy:** Open-source companies have fundamentally different portfolio structures from proprietary SaaS. The core product is freely available — monetization wraps around it via enterprise features, managed cloud services, support subscriptions, and licensing strategy. Community health and contributor ecosystem are first-class portfolio dimensions. The open core vs. dual-license vs. BSL decision IS the business model, not just a pricing detail.

---

## Dimension Definitions

### 0. Provider Profile Metrics

**Domain:** Organizational business KPIs and market presence

**Core Question:** *"What are the provider's key business indicators, scale, and market positioning?"*

**Focus:** Annual revenue/ARR, employee count, community size, geographic presence, market rankings, certifications, partnership tiers

**MECE Role:** Covers foundational provider context (who they are) — orthogonal to platform dimensions 1-7 (what they offer)

---

### 1. Open Source Projects

**Domain:** The open-source projects that form the foundation of the commercial offering

**Core Question:** *"What open-source projects does the company maintain, and what is their community health and maturity?"*

**Focus:** Core OSS project(s), contributor ecosystem metrics, release governance, foundation graduation status (CNCF, ASF), community distributions, fork compatibility, open standards adherence

**MECE Role:** Covers the open-source foundation — the freely available assets the business is built on

---

### 2. Enterprise Platform

**Domain:** Commercial features and enterprise-grade capabilities beyond the OSS core

**Core Question:** *"What enterprise features, hardening, and capabilities are available beyond the open-source version?"*

**Focus:** Enterprise edition features, security hardening (FIPS, SSO), high availability, multi-tenancy, admin/governance consoles, enterprise integrations, air-gapped deployment

**MECE Role:** Covers the commercial value-add layer that differentiates paid offerings from the free OSS core

---

### 3. Cloud & Managed Services

**Domain:** Hosted, managed, and cloud-native deployment options

**Core Question:** *"What cloud-managed and hosted versions of the platform are available?"*

**Focus:** Fully managed cloud (DBaaS/PaaS), serverless tiers, self-hosted with vendor support, hybrid cloud, marketplace listings (AWS/Azure/GCP), edge deployment

**MECE Role:** Covers all cloud delivery models — from fully managed to self-hosted with support

---

### 4. Developer Ecosystem

**Domain:** Developer experience, tooling, and community resources

**Core Question:** *"What developer tools, documentation, and community resources does the platform provide?"*

**Focus:** Documentation quality, SDKs and client libraries, plugin/extension marketplace, developer advocacy, certification programs, ISV partner ecosystem

**MECE Role:** Covers all developer-facing resources and community engagement

---

### 5. Support & Subscriptions

**Domain:** Support models, SLAs, and subscription-based commercial offerings

**Core Question:** *"What support tiers, SLA levels, and subscription offerings are available?"*

**Focus:** Support tiers (community/standard/premium), SLA guarantees, incident response, LTS releases, consulting credits, technical account management

**MECE Role:** Covers all recurring subscription and support services

---

### 6. Professional Services

**Domain:** Implementation, advisory, and hands-on delivery services

**Core Question:** *"What professional services are available for implementation, optimization, and enablement?"*

**Focus:** Implementation and migration services, architecture review, performance tuning, training programs, managed operations, custom development

**MECE Role:** Covers all project-based and ongoing professional services

---

### 7. Licensing & Monetization

**Domain:** License models, usage tracking, and commercial terms

**Core Question:** *"What is the licensing model, and how is the commercial offering monetized?"*

**Focus:** License type (Apache/GPL/SSPL/BSL/dual), usage metering, community edition scope, commercial terms, audit tools, OEM/embedded licensing

**MECE Role:** Covers the licensing and monetization strategy — the business model architecture itself

## IS/DOES/MEANS Pattern for Open-Source

Open-source propositions follow a distinctive messaging pattern:

- **IS** (capability): The OSS project itself — freely available, community-proven, standards-compliant
- **DOES** (advantage): The commercial wrapper — enterprise security, managed operations, guaranteed SLAs, professional support
- **MEANS** (outcome): Reduced operational risk, faster time-to-value than DIY OSS, vendor-backed innovation without lock-in

This "free core + commercial value-add" pattern should inform proposition generation for all COSS companies.

## MECE Validation

**Mutually Exclusive:**

- Dimension 0 (provider profile) vs. Dimensions 1-7 (portfolio offerings) are categorically distinct
- Open Source Projects (the free foundation) vs. Enterprise Platform (the commercial wrapper) — clear boundary at the license gate
- Cloud & Managed Services (how it's deployed/operated) vs. Support & Subscriptions (what guarantees come with it)
- Developer Ecosystem (community/developer resources) vs. Professional Services (paid expert delivery)
- Licensing & Monetization (business model architecture) is orthogonal to all delivery dimensions

**Collectively Exhaustive:**

- Provider Profile Metrics — Company context and market presence
- Open Source Projects — The OSS foundation and community health
- Enterprise Platform — Commercial features beyond OSS
- Cloud & Managed Services — All deployment and hosting models
- Developer Ecosystem — All developer-facing resources
- Support & Subscriptions — All recurring support services
- Professional Services — All project-based expert delivery
- Licensing & Monetization — The business model itself

Together these 8 dimensions (0-7) cover both provider context and the complete COSS portfolio scope.
