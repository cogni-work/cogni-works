## Emerging Signals

The following candidates were not assigned to any strategic theme. They represent early signals worth monitoring — their absence from current themes may itself be informative.

### Wasserknappheit & Netzoper. (T)

Climate-driven water scarcity threatens cooling water supply for thermal power plants and data centers through 2032. While not yet integrated into a strategic theme, this signal may gain urgency as climate adaptation requirements intensify. Horizon: OBSERVE.

### Vergabeschwellen-Anpassung (T)

EU procurement thresholds updated for 2026-2027 affect how municipal utilities participate in public procurement. Primarily a compliance topic without strategic implications at this stage. Horizon: OBSERVE.

### Netz-Resilienz Post-Extremwetter (T)

Extreme weather events drive resilience investments in grid infrastructure redundancy. This signal may warrant its own theme as climate adaptation costs escalate and regulators impose resilience mandates. Horizon: OBSERVE.

### Carbon-Pricing-Expansion (T)

ETS2 expansion to buildings and transport from 2028 covers 75% of EU emissions. While partially addressed in Theme 5, the customer-facing implications (tariff restructuring, green product positioning) cross into Theme 3 territory. Horizon: PLAN.

### Engie UK Power Networks M&A (P)

ENGIE's £10.5B acquisition of UKPN signals European network consolidation. This M&A trend may warrant a separate strategic assessment for German utilities evaluating consolidation or partnership strategies. Horizon: ACT.

### Klir Series B Data Hub (P)

Klir's €17.5M Series B for utility data hub solutions represents the startup ecosystem developing specialized utility platforms. Worth monitoring for technology partnerships. Horizon: OBSERVE.

### Agile Betriebsmodelle (P)

Shift toward decentralized, agile operating models is a long-horizon organizational trend that underpins the execution capability for all five themes. Horizon: OBSERVE.
