# B2B Industrial Technology Portfolio Taxonomy

Single source of truth for the B2B Industrial Technology Portfolio framework — 8 dimensions (0-7), 48 categories.

## Purpose

The framework provides a MECE structure for analyzing B2B industrial technology companies. Dimension 0 covers provider business profile metrics; Dimensions 1-7 cover automation platforms, manufacturing execution, digital twin, industrial IoT, OT cybersecurity, lifecycle services, and engineering advisory.

---

## Dimension Definitions

### 0. Provider Profile Metrics

**Domain:** Organizational business KPIs and market presence

**Core Question:** *"What are the provider's key business indicators, scale, and market positioning?"*

**Focus:** Revenue scale, employee count, geographic presence, market rankings, certifications, partnership ecosystem

**MECE Role:** Covers foundational provider context (who they are) — orthogonal to capability dimensions 1-7 (what they offer)

---

### 1. Automation & Control

**Domain:** Industrial automation hardware and software for process and discrete manufacturing

**Core Question:** *"What automation and control systems does the provider offer for industrial processes?"*

**Focus:** PLCs, DCS, SCADA/HMI, motion control, robotics, instrumentation, industrial networking, edge computing, safety systems

**MECE Role:** Covers the foundational control-layer capabilities — the automation backbone of any industrial operation

---

### 2. Manufacturing Execution

**Domain:** Shop-floor operations management and production optimization

**Core Question:** *"What manufacturing execution and operations management capabilities does the provider offer?"*

**Focus:** MES/MOM platforms, quality management, production scheduling, track & trace, recipe/batch management, warehouse execution

**MECE Role:** Covers all shop-floor software that bridges the gap between automation (Dim 1) and enterprise systems

---

### 3. Digital Twin & Simulation

**Domain:** Virtual representations of products, processes, and assets

**Core Question:** *"What digital twin and simulation capabilities does the provider offer for design, commissioning, and operations?"*

**Focus:** Product digital twin, process digital twin, asset digital twin, simulation & modeling, virtual commissioning, AR/VR for operations

**MECE Role:** Covers all virtual modeling and simulation — distinct from live operational data (Dim 4) and physical control (Dim 1)

---

### 4. Industrial IoT & Data

**Domain:** Connected operations, data management, and analytics for industrial assets

**Core Question:** *"What Industrial IoT platform, data management, and analytics capabilities does the provider offer?"*

**Focus:** IoT platform, industrial data management, predictive analytics, condition monitoring, energy management, emissions monitoring

**MECE Role:** Covers all data-layer capabilities for live operational insight — distinct from simulation (Dim 3) and control (Dim 1)

---

### 5. OT Cybersecurity

**Domain:** Security of operational technology networks, assets, and industrial control systems

**Core Question:** *"What OT cybersecurity capabilities does the provider offer to protect industrial infrastructure?"*

**Focus:** OT network security, asset discovery & visibility, OT SOC, ICS vulnerability management, IEC 62443 compliance, secure remote access

**MECE Role:** Covers all security and compliance for the OT environment — orthogonal to functional capabilities

---

### 6. Lifecycle & Service

**Domain:** Asset lifecycle management and industrial service delivery

**Core Question:** *"What lifecycle management and service capabilities does the provider offer for installed base?"*

**Focus:** Asset lifecycle management, predictive/preventive maintenance, field service management, spare parts management, remote monitoring & diagnostics

**MECE Role:** Covers all post-commissioning service capabilities — distinct from initial engineering (Dim 7) and live data (Dim 4)

---

### 7. Engineering & Advisory

**Domain:** Plant engineering, digital transformation consulting, and managed operations

**Core Question:** *"What engineering, consulting, and advisory services does the provider offer?"*

**Focus:** Plant engineering & design, digital transformation consulting, OT system integration, training & certification, managed OT operations, sustainability & decarbonization advisory

**MECE Role:** Covers all human-delivered engineering and advisory services — distinct from product capabilities in Dimensions 1-6

---

## MECE Validation

**Mutually Exclusive:**

- Dimension 0 (provider profile) vs. Dimensions 1-7 (capabilities and services) are categorically distinct
- Automation & Control (physical control layer) vs. Manufacturing Execution (shop-floor software) vs. Digital Twin (virtual models)
- Industrial IoT & Data (live operational data) vs. Digital Twin & Simulation (virtual models) — data feeds twins, but capabilities are distinct
- OT Cybersecurity (security/governance) is orthogonal to all functional capability dimensions
- Lifecycle & Service (post-commissioning) vs. Engineering & Advisory (pre-commissioning and consulting) are distinct service phases

**Collectively Exhaustive:**

- Provider Profile Metrics — Company context and market presence
- Automation & Control — Physical control-layer hardware and software
- Manufacturing Execution — Shop-floor operations management
- Digital Twin & Simulation — Virtual modeling and simulation
- Industrial IoT & Data — Connected operations and analytics
- OT Cybersecurity — Security of industrial infrastructure
- Lifecycle & Service — Post-commissioning service delivery
- Engineering & Advisory — Engineering, consulting, and managed services

Together these 8 dimensions (0-7) cover both provider context and the complete B2B industrial technology portfolio scope.
