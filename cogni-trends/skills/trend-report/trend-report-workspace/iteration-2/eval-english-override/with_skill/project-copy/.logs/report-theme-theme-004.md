## 4. Cybersecurity & Regulatorische Daten-Souveränität

> How do we protect critical energy infrastructure and ensure data sovereignty under tightening cybersecurity and privacy requirements?

**Executive Sponsor:** CISO / VP Informationssicherheit

### Investment Thesis

The grid cybersecurity market is projected to grow from $9.11 billion in 2025 to $10.43 billion in 2026, reaching $29.70 billion by 2034 — a 14.4% CAGR that reflects both the scale of the threat and the regulatory response [Precedence Research](https://www.precedenceresearch.com/grid-cybersecurity-market). Zero Trust Architecture is becoming the predominant security paradigm for critical energy infrastructure, requiring continuous authentication and micro-segmentation across all grid environments [GM Insights](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/smart-grid-cybersecurity-market).

The convergence of cybersecurity mandates and extended GDPR regulations for real-time energy data creates a dual compliance challenge. Utilities must protect KRITIS infrastructure against increasingly sophisticated threats while simultaneously implementing granular consent management for millions of smart meter data points. This is not merely a defensive investment — utilities that achieve security maturity gain strategic advantages through performance-based regulation (adopted by 28+ US states and moving to Europe [Strategy&](https://www.strategyand.pwc.com/gx/en/industries/power-utilities/utilities-innovation.html)), reduced insurance premiums, and trusted partnerships with hyperscalers for sovereign data center operations.

The talent dimension compounds the challenge: AI/ML hiring growing 88% YoY and the broader workforce shortage create fierce competition for security professionals. Building internal capability through upskilling and managed security service partnerships is essential.

### Strategic Actions

1. **Implement zero-trust architecture for OT/IT segmentation** — Network segmentation between IT and OT is the foundational security investment.
2. **Deploy AI-powered threat detection for grid infrastructure** — $10.43B market in 2026 reflects maturity of available solutions.
3. **Build sovereign data center partnerships** — Combine KRITIS compliance with hyperscaler capabilities for data-intensive workloads.
4. **Implement granular consent management for smart meter data** — GDPR extension requires device-level consent tracking.
