## 1. Intelligente Netz- & Asset-Optimierung

> How do we digitalize grid operations and asset management to handle growing demand, renewable integration, and plant retirements?

**Executive Sponsor:** CTO / Leiter Netzbetrieb

### Investment Thesis

The convergence of three external forces — data center demand surges, accelerating renewable curtailment, and massive fossil capacity retirements — is creating an existential challenge for traditional grid operations. U.S. data center demand alone is projected to reach 75.8 GW in 2026, with five-year peak demand forecasts jumping from 38 GW to 128 GW in a single year [WRI](https://www.wri.org/insights/us-data-centers-electricity-demand). In Germany, the situation compounds: solar curtailment surged 97% year-on-year in 2024 to 1,389 GWh [Strategic Energy Europe](https://strategicenergy.eu/solar-curtailment-germany/), while the EU retired 11 GW of coal capacity in 2024 alone — a fourfold increase over 2023 [Global Energy Monitor](https://globalenergymonitor.org/report/boom-and-bust-coal-2025/).

This demand-supply imbalance cannot be solved by building more physical infrastructure alone. Grid-enhancing technologies offer a faster path: the IEA estimates GETs could unlock 1,200–1,600 GW of hosting capacity for projects stuck in interconnection queues worldwide [IEA](https://www.iea.org/reports/electricity-2026/grids). The DOE has committed $1.9 billion in funding for grid upgrades [Renewable Energy World](https://www.renewableenergyworld.com/power-grid/grid-modernization/breaking-doe-rolls-out-1-9b-funding-opportunity-for-urgently-needed-grid-upgrades/), and by mid-2025, at least 14 US states had enacted GET evaluation legislation.

Smart metering provides the data foundation for intelligent grid operations: global revenues could reach $20 billion by 2026 [MIT Sloan](https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/smart-meters-generate-revenue-improve-efficiency-public-utilities), and automated workflows achieve 30% reduction in critical event escalations. Meanwhile, the predictive maintenance market in energy is growing at 25.77% CAGR to $7.08 billion by 2030, with NextEra Energy's program delivering 23% outage reduction and $25 million annual savings [Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/predictive-maintenance-in-energy-market). The overall investment context is massive: the top 40 European utilities will invest €173 billion in 2026 [ING Think](https://think.ing.com/articles/energy-european-utilities-2026-steady-currents/), and global grid digitalization investment reaches $713 billion over 6 years [Datacenters.com](https://www.datacenters.com/news/the-future-of-technology-infrastructure-trends-to-watch-through-2026).

The strategic imperative is clear: make the grid smarter, not just bigger. Digital twins, predictive maintenance, and grid-enhancing technologies can deliver 10–15% capacity increases without physical expansion — but only if utilities invest now in the foundational data platforms and legacy modernization that enable these capabilities.

### Strategic Actions

1. **Deploy grid-enhancing technologies at top-10 congestion points** — GETs deliver 10–15% capacity gains within 12 months, the fastest ROI path for demand growth pressure.
2. **Launch a Smart Grid Digital Twin pilot for one network region** — Validated by NextEra's $25M annual savings, digital twins transform reactive maintenance into predictive operations.
3. **Implement smart meter rollout acceleration program** — $20B global market signals the platform is mature; smart metering is the data foundation for all other theme capabilities.
4. **Establish sovereign cloud infrastructure for grid workloads** — KRITIS compliance and AI model training both require hybrid cloud with European data sovereignty.
5. **Initiate legacy OT modernization with API-first architecture** — 75% of enterprises cite legacy systems as the barrier; GenAI can cut migration timelines by 50%.
