# Arc Selection Guide

Narrative arcs determine the structural progression of each insight summary. The arc frames how evidence flows — a technology arc emphasizes emergence and convergence, while a competitive arc emphasizes positioning and implications. All arcs produce the same quality output; the difference is which aspects of the research get foregrounded.

## Recommended Arcs by Research Type

| Research Type | Recommended Arc | Narrative Progression |
|---|---|---|
| generic | corporate-visions | Why Change → Why Now → Why You → Why Pay |
| generic (tech-focused) | technology-futures | Emerging → Converging → Possible → Required |
| generic (competitive) | competitive-intelligence | Landscape → Shifts → Positioning → Implications |
| lean-canvas | corporate-visions | Maps well to business model validation narrative |
| b2b-ict-portfolio | technology-futures | Maps ICT portfolio dimensions to technology evolution |
| b2b-ict-portfolio (market) | competitive-intelligence | Market positioning and competitive analysis |

## How to Choose

1. **Check `research_type`** in sprint-log — this gives the default recommendation from the table above.
2. **Consider the audience** — if the research is for a sales team, `corporate-visions` tends to resonate best. For R&D or strategy teams, `technology-futures` works better.
3. **Consider the research question** — competitive questions naturally fit `competitive-intelligence`, even if the research type is generic.

When in doubt, ask the user. Present the auto-detected recommendation alongside alternatives so they can make an informed choice.
