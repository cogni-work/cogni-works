## I — Implications: Digital Value Drivers

### ACT Horizon (0-2 Years)

#### 1. AI-Accelerated Incident Resolution

**Trend Overview** — AI-equipped incident response teams are accelerating resolution times dramatically. Enterprise organizations using AI-driven observability report MTTR reductions of 40–60%, with some platforms achieving up to 70% improvement [Rootly](https://rootly.com/sre/best-ai-sre-tools-for-faster-incident-resolution-in-2026). In 2026, AIOps has evolved from anomaly detection to autonomous, agentic operations that explain root causes, suggest fixes, and in advanced cases, heal systems automatically [IR.com](https://www.ir.com/guides/how-to-reduce-mttr-with-ai-a-2026-guide-for-enterprise-it-teams).

**Implications** — Utilities deploying AI incident management can dramatically reduce outage duration and associated revenue losses across both IT and OT environments.

**Opportunities** — AI-powered incident response enables 24/7 autonomous monitoring with predictive alerting, reducing staffing requirements during off-peak hours.

**Recommended Actions** — 1. Deploy AI-powered incident detection across critical grid systems. 2. Implement automated diagnosis and remediation workflows. 3. Establish AIOps command centers integrating IT and OT monitoring.

#### 2. Digital Customer Experience (616/1000)

**Trend Overview** — The 2026 JD Power U.S. Utility Digital Experience Study reveals overall customer satisfaction at 616 on a 1,000-point scale, up 5 points from last year. Mobile apps lead with 654 average satisfaction — 63 points higher than mobile websites (591). A 100-point gap separates the top-ranked utility (660) from the bottom (560) [JD Power](https://www.jdpower.com/business/press-releases/2026-us-utility-digital-experience-study). Notably, 28% of utilities still do not offer mobile apps.

**Implications** — The 100-point gap between leaders and laggards indicates massive optimization potential. Navigation difficulties in pricing plans, rebates, and outage information represent priority improvement areas.

**Opportunities** — Utilities investing in mobile-first digital experiences can close the satisfaction gap and reduce customer churn.

**Recommended Actions** — 1. Launch or upgrade utility mobile apps with modern UX. 2. Prioritize outage viewing, pricing plan, and rebate navigation improvements. 3. Implement A/B testing for continuous digital experience optimization.

### PLAN Horizon (2-5 Years)

#### 1. AI Agent Workflow Automation

**Trend Overview** — AI agents are expected to automate 30–50% of utility workflows by 2027–2028, with measurable improvements in engineering efficiency and quality [XenonStack](https://www.xenonstack.com/blog/agentic-ai-energy-sector). AI is entering a more operational phase in 2026, with companies integrating AI into core operations across energy systems [Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/transform/power-energy-companies-innovating-with-ai-and-cloud-2026-industry-tech-trends). However, Gartner predicts over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027.

**Implications** — Successful AI agent deployment requires a systematic approach: identify automatable processes, build governance frameworks, and manage organizational change simultaneously.

**Opportunities** — Process areas with highest automation potential include customer service, billing, network planning, and field dispatch.

**Recommended Actions** — 1. Identify and prioritize automatable workflows by ROI. 2. Deploy AI agent platforms with human-in-the-loop governance. 3. Develop change management programs for affected workforce.
