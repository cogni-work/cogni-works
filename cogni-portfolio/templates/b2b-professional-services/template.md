# B2B Professional Services Portfolio Taxonomy

Single source of truth for the B2B Professional Services Portfolio framework — 8 dimensions (0-7), 44 categories.

## Purpose

The framework provides a MECE structure for analyzing B2B professional services firms. Dimension 0 covers provider business profile metrics; Dimensions 1-7 cover advisory capabilities, industry practices, engagement models, and go-to-market.

---

## Dimension Definitions

### 0. Provider Profile Metrics

**Domain:** Organizational business KPIs and market presence

**Core Question:** *"What are the provider's key business indicators, scale, and market positioning?"*

**Focus:** Revenue, employee count, geographic presence, market rankings, certifications, partnership ecosystem

**MECE Role:** Covers foundational provider context (who they are) — orthogonal to capability dimensions 1-7 (what they offer)

---

### 1. Strategy & Transformation

**Domain:** Strategic advisory and organizational change

**Core Question:** *"What strategic advisory, transformation design, and growth strategy capabilities does the firm offer?"*

**Focus:** Corporate strategy, operating model design, M&A advisory, organizational transformation, innovation strategy, ESG strategy

**MECE Role:** Covers high-level strategic advisory — what direction the client should take

---

### 2. Operations & Performance

**Domain:** Operational improvement and cost optimization

**Core Question:** *"What operational advisory capabilities does the firm offer to improve performance, efficiency, and resilience?"*

**Focus:** Supply chain optimization, process excellence, procurement advisory, cost transformation, operational risk management

**MECE Role:** Covers how the client should run its operations — distinct from strategy (direction) and technology (enablers)

---

### 3. Technology & Digital Advisory

**Domain:** Technology strategy and digital enablement

**Core Question:** *"What technology strategy, digital transformation, and technical advisory capabilities does the firm offer?"*

**Focus:** Digital strategy, technology selection and architecture, data and AI advisory, cybersecurity advisory, cloud strategy

**MECE Role:** Covers technology-focused advisory — the digital and IT enablement layer

---

### 4. Industry Practices

**Domain:** Vertical-specific expertise and sector-focused advisory

**Core Question:** *"What industry-specific practices, sector expertise, and vertical solutions does the firm maintain?"*

**Focus:** Financial services, healthcare and life sciences, energy and resources, public sector, manufacturing and industrial, TMT

**MECE Role:** Covers all vertical specialization — orthogonal to horizontal capability dimensions 1-3 and 5-7

---

### 5. Risk, Compliance & Assurance

**Domain:** Risk management, regulatory compliance, and assurance services

**Core Question:** *"What risk, compliance, audit, and assurance capabilities does the firm offer?"*

**Focus:** Regulatory advisory, internal audit, financial audit support, forensic and investigation, enterprise risk management, third-party risk

**MECE Role:** Covers all governance, risk, and compliance advisory — distinct from operational risk (Dim 2) which focuses on process-level risks

---

### 6. People & Change

**Domain:** Human capital, organizational change, and workforce advisory

**Core Question:** *"What people strategy, change management, and workforce advisory capabilities does the firm offer?"*

**Focus:** Change management, talent and workforce advisory, leadership development, HR transformation, culture and engagement

**MECE Role:** Covers all human-capital and organizational-change advisory — distinct from strategy (Dim 1) which sets direction, and operations (Dim 2) which optimizes processes

---

### 7. Engagement Models

**Domain:** Commercial delivery models and contracting approaches

**Core Question:** *"What engagement models, delivery formats, and commercial structures does the firm offer?"*

**Focus:** Retainer/advisory, project-based delivery, managed services, secondment/staff augmentation, outcome-based engagements

**MECE Role:** Covers how the firm delivers and prices its work — orthogonal to what it delivers (Dims 1-6)

---

## Service Horizons

Each capability is positioned by market maturity and availability:

| Horizon | Timeframe | Characteristics |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Current Offerings** | 0-1 years | Generally available, proven engagements, established fee structures |
| **Emerging Services** | 1-3 years | Pilot programs, limited availability, evolving pricing |
| **Future Roadmap** | 3+ years | Announced, conceptual, R&D phase, no fixed pricing |

## MECE Validation

**Mutually Exclusive:**

- Dimension 0 (provider profile) vs. Dimensions 1-7 (service capabilities) are categorically distinct
- Strategy & Transformation (what direction) vs. Operations & Performance (how to run) vs. Technology & Digital (what enables)
- Risk, Compliance & Assurance (governance/trust) is orthogonal to functional advisory capabilities
- People & Change (human capital) vs. Strategy (direction) vs. Operations (processes) are distinct advisory domains
- Industry Practices (vertical expertise) vs. Dimensions 1-3 and 5-7 (horizontal capabilities)
- Engagement Models (how delivered) vs. Dimensions 1-6 (what is delivered)

**Collectively Exhaustive:**

- Provider Profile Metrics — Firm context and market presence
- Strategy & Transformation — Strategic direction and large-scale change
- Operations & Performance — Operational efficiency and cost optimization
- Technology & Digital Advisory — Technology enablement and digital strategy
- Industry Practices — Vertical-specific expertise and sector solutions
- Risk, Compliance & Assurance — Governance, risk, and regulatory compliance
- People & Change — Human capital and organizational change
- Engagement Models — Delivery formats and commercial structures

Together these 8 dimensions (0-7) cover both provider context and the complete B2B professional services portfolio scope.
